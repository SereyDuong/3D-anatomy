Our Category

Heart

Your muscular heart, the main organ in your cardiovascular system, is vital for life. Its parts work together to move blood through your body in a coordinated way. It constantly sends oxygen to your cells and takes away waste. Many conditions can affect this organ and keep it from working well.

Learn More >>

Brain

Your brain is an essential organ that controls many body functions. Your brain receives and interprets all the sensory information you encounter, like sights, sounds, smells and tastes. Your brain has many complex parts that work together to help you function.

Learn More >>

Lungs

Your two lungs make oxygen available to your body and remove gases like carbon dioxide. There are many conditions that can affect your lungs. Not smoking may help you prevent some illnesses.

Learn More >>

Liver

The liver is a large organ in the abdomen that performs many important bodily functions, including blood filtering. It is also considered a gland because it makes chemicals the body needs. Certain diseases and lifestyles can damage the liver, but there are many ways to protect this vital organ.

Learn More >>

Stomach

Your stomach is a muscular organ that digests food. It is part of your gastrointestinal (GI) tract. When your stomach receives food, it contracts and produces acids and enzymes that break down food. When your stomach has broken down food, it passes it to your small intestine.

Learn More >>

Kidney

Most people have two kidneys, organs that sit in the back of your abdomen. Your kidneys’ primary function is to filter your blood. They also remove waste and balance your body’s fluids. Common kidney conditions include kidney disease, kidney infections and kidney cysts.

Learn More >>

Colon

The large intestine includes the colon, rectum and anus. It’s all one, long tube that continues from the small intestine as food nears the end of its journey through your digestive system. The large intestine turns food waste into stool and passes it from the body when you poop.

Learn More >>

Small Bowel

The small intestine is actually the longest segment of the gastrointestinal tract — the long, continuous pathway that food travels through your digestive system. In the small intestine, food is broken down into liquid and most of its nutrients are absorbed. The waste is passed on to the large intestine.

Frequently asked questions

What is human anatomy, and why is it important?

Human anatomy is the study of the structure and organization of the human body. Understanding anatomy is crucial for medical professionals, students, and anyone interested in health and biology.

What topics do you cover?

We cover the structure, function, and importance of internal organs such as the heart, lungs, liver, kidneys, brain, and digestive system.

Do you offer interactive learning tools?

Yes, we provide diagrams, 3D models, quizzes, and case studies to enhance learning.

What is the most important organ in the body?

Every organ plays a crucial role, but the brain controls body functions, and the heart pumps blood, making them essential for survival.

How can I keep my internal organs healthy?

Eating a balanced diet, exercising, staying hydrated, and avoiding harmful substances like alcohol and tobacco help maintain organ health.

How can I ask questions or request specific topics?

You can contact us through our inquiry form or participate in our discussion forums.
